# Module 1: Introduction to AI

## 📚 Overview

This module provides a comprehensive introduction to Artificial Intelligence, covering:
- What is AI and its definitions
- Historical evolution and major milestones
- Types of AI and their differences
- Real-world applications
- Ethical considerations

## 📖 Topics

### 1. What is AI?
- Definitions and scope
- AI vs. Machine Learning vs. Deep Learning
- Strong AI vs. Weak AI
- Turing Test

### 2. History of AI
- Birth of AI (1956 Dartmouth Conference)
- Early enthusiasm and AI winters
- Expert systems era
- Machine learning renaissance
- Deep learning revolution
- Modern era and beyond

### 3. Types of AI
- **Narrow AI (Weak AI)**: Designed for specific tasks
- **General AI (Strong AI)**: Hypothetical AI with human-level intelligence
- **Super AI (ASI)**: Hypothetical AI surpassing human intelligence

### 4. AI Capabilities
- Perception (vision, speech)
- Reasoning and problem-solving
- Learning from data
- Natural language understanding
- Planning and decision-making

### 5. AI Applications
- Healthcare (diagnosis, drug discovery)
- Finance (fraud detection, trading)
- Transportation (autonomous vehicles)
- Entertainment (recommendations, games)
- Manufacturing (optimization, quality control)
- Education (personalized learning)
- Agriculture (crop monitoring, yield prediction)

### 6. Ethics in AI
- Bias and fairness
- Privacy and security
- Accountability and transparency
- Societal impact
- Responsible AI

## 🔧 Key Concepts

| Concept | Definition | Example |
|---------|-----------|----------|
| **Algorithm** | Step-by-step procedure to solve a problem | BFS, A* search |
| **Training Data** | Data used to teach AI systems | Image dataset, historical records |
| **Model** | Learned pattern from training | Neural network, decision tree |
| **Inference** | Using trained model to make predictions | Classifying new images |
| **Bias** | Systematic errors in AI predictions | Gender bias in hiring AI |
| **Overfitting** | Model memorizes instead of generalizing | Low train error, high test error |
| **Generalization** | Model works well on unseen data | Consistent train and test error |
