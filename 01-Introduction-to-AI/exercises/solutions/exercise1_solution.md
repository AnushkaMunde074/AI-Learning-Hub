# Exercise 1: Solution - Understanding AI Types

## Classifications

### Narrow AI
1. **Deep Blue** - Plays chess only
3. **Spam filter** - Detects spam emails
5. **Medical diagnosis system** - Specific diseases only
7. **Netflix recommendation** - Recommends movies/shows

### General AI
2. **Hypothetical AI** - Any intellectual task
4. **Autonomous vehicle** - Actually still Narrow! (designed for driving)
6. **Personal assistant** - Can help with any task (hypothetical today)
8. **Learn new skills** - True General AI capability

## Answers

### 1. Key Differences

**Narrow AI:**
- Designed for specific task
- Cannot transfer learning to other domains
- All AI systems today belong here
- Examples: ChatGPT (for language), AlphaGo (for Go)

**General AI:**
- Can perform any intellectual task
- Can transfer knowledge between domains
- Hypothetical/theoretical today
- Would require common sense reasoning

### 2. Capabilities for General AI

Would need:
- ✅ Common sense reasoning
- ✅ Transfer learning across domains
- ✅ Learning from minimal data (few-shot learning)
- ✅ Reasoning about physical world
- ✅ Understanding context and nuance
- ✅ Adapting to new situations
- ✅ Metacognition (thinking about thinking)

### 3. Why General AI is Still Theoretical

**Challenges:**
1. **Data Requirements**: General AI would need understanding of everything
2. **Computational Power**: Might require more than we can build
3. **Common Sense**: Hard to teach machines about real world
4. **Transfer Learning**: Not solved at human level
5. **Interpretability**: We don't understand our own intelligence
6. **Safety**: Creating powerful AI raises safety concerns

### 4. Can Narrow AI Become General AI?

**Answer:** Not by simply scaling current approaches.

**Reasoning:**
- Current AI learns patterns in specific domains
- Doesn't transfer to new domains without retraining
- Would need fundamentally new approaches
- Possibly requires combining multiple Narrow AIs
- May need new architectures and learning paradigms

**Possibilities:**
- ✅ Build meta-learning systems that learn how to learn
- ✅ Create world models that reason about physics
- ✅ Combine multiple specialized systems
- ✅ Use multimodal learning (vision, language, reasoning)

### 5. Challenges to Building General AI

**Technical:**
- Common sense reasoning unsolved
- Transfer learning at human level not achieved
- Computational efficiency unclear
- Data requirements potentially infinite

**Theoretical:**
- Understanding human intelligence incomplete
- Multiple learning paradigms needed
- Integration of reasoning and learning
- Handling uncertainty and incomplete information

**Practical:**
- Safety and alignment concerns
- Regulatory and ethical frameworks lacking
- Massive computational resources needed
- Current architectures may be insufficient

## Additional Analysis

### Correction Note
Autonomous vehicles are technically Narrow AI because they're:
- Specialized for driving tasks
- Not capable of other intellectual tasks
- Would need separate systems for communication, reasoning, etc.

However, combining autonomous driving with other systems approaches General AI!

## Key Takeaways

1. **All today's AI is Narrow AI** - specialized for specific tasks
2. **General AI remains theoretical** - major unsolved problems
3. **Different approaches might be needed** - current methods may not scale
4. **Progress is being made** - transfer learning, meta-learning improving
5. **Long-term horizon** - Decades away, if possible at all
